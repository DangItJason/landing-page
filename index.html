What is Emi?

The Truth About Caregiving in America

You're managing medication schedules at 3 AM while your loved one sleeps fitfully. Your phone buzzes with appointment reminders you forgot to set. The insurance company wants forms you can't find. Your boss keeps asking when you'll be "back to normal." Meanwhile, you haven't slept through the night in months, and that crushing weight in your chest grows heavier each day.

This is caregiving in America. Not the sanitized version you see in commercials with smiling families and gentle piano music. The real version where 53 million Americans are drowning in a system that expects miracles from exhausted people. Where you're supposed to become a medical expert, insurance navigator, and emotional support system overnight – all while pretending your own life isn't falling apart.

Caregiving is f***ing hard. And anyone who tells you otherwise has never spent their lunch break on hold with Medicare while their parent forgets to eat lunch again.

How Emi Changes Everything

Your brain is already maxed out. Emi handles the mental load – tracking medications, remembering what the doctor said last Tuesday, knowing which pharmacy has the prescription ready. She creates order from the chaos swirling in your head.

She Speaks Healthcare Fluency
Insurance denials written in medical hieroglyphics? Emi translates. Doctor's notes that might as well be in ancient Greek? She breaks it down. That pile of paperwork staring at you from the kitchen counter? She knows exactly what each form means and what you need to do next.

She Plans Ahead So You Can Breathe
Emi doesn't just react to crises – she prevents them. She spots patterns in symptoms before they become emergencies. She reminds you about prescription refills before you're scrambling at midnight. She helps you prepare for appointments so you walk in confident instead of frazzled.

She Connects You With Real Help
When you need human support, Emi knows exactly who to connect you with. The right specialist for your specific situation. Support groups where people actually understand. Resources in your area that don't require a PhD to navigate.

Built for the Reality of Caregiving

Emi understands that caregiving happens in stolen moments between everything else. She works while you're commuting, sleeping, or finally taking that shower you've been putting off for three days. She's there at 2 AM when anxiety hits and you need someone who gets it.

This isn't about replacing human connection – it's about giving you the support and systems you need to show up fully for the people and moments that matter most.

Because you shouldn't have to choose between taking care of someone you love and taking care of yourself.

About EmiCare

Our Story

At just 23 years old, Jason began his caregiving journey when he moved in with his grandmother. At an age when most are launching their careers and personal lives, he found himself juggling the complex emotional and logistical responsibilities of caring for an elderly loved one.

The early days were overwhelming, marked by exhaustion, frustration, and even resentment. "Why couldn't she find her phone for the tenth time? Why did she rely on me for every task?" These questions often dominated his thoughts. Yet behind those frustrations was a deeper truth: caregiving was about love, connection, and sharing life's moments—even the challenging ones.

A profound turning point arrived during a deeply personal psychedelic experience. For the first time, he truly saw his grandmother—not as a frail, elderly woman, but as a strong, resilient person navigating the loneliness and physical struggles of aging. Witnessing her silently bear the daily burdens—like preparing multiple meals just to conserve energy—he realized caregiving wasn't just a duty, it was an opportunity to give back the love and compassion she had always provided to him. At that moment, caregiving transformed from a burden to an act of profound love and compassion.

Our Mission

At EmiCare, we deeply understand the emotional rollercoaster caregivers experience—the loneliness, the endless multitasking, and the critical decisions made on behalf of loved ones. Our mission is clear: we exist to transform caregiving from an overwhelming task into an act of love. EmiCare is designed to be the caregiving companion you've always wished for.

What Emi Does

Think of Emi as the care coordinator you wish you had access to at 2 AM. She's available 24/7 to help you figure out what the hell you're supposed to do next – whether that's understanding an insurance denial, finding a home health aide, or just knowing if that new symptom is worth a 3 AM call to the doctor.

Here's what makes Emi different from the million other apps that promise to "solve" caregiving:

  • She handles the stuff you don't have time for: Scheduling appointments, decoding insurance paperwork, finding local resources, setting up medication reminders, and yes – even providing emotional encouragement when you're having one of those days where nothing goes right.
  • She knows when you need a real person: Emi can handle the daily grind – tracking symptoms, answering routine questions, managing schedules. But when something serious comes up, she connects you with actual nurses and social workers who know what they're talking about.
  • She's there when no one else is: Your mom's doctor isn't available at midnight when she's confused about her medication. The insurance company is closed when you finally have time to deal with that claim. Emi is always there, and she remembers everything about your specific situation.
  • She connects your entire care network: Your siblings, close friends, the home health aide, physical therapist – whoever is involved in care can connect with Emi. Everyone stays informed about what's actually happening instead of playing telephone with partial information. No more "I thought you were handling that" conversations or your PT asking about changes nobody told them about.

Look, we're not trying to replace human connection or medical professionals. We're trying to handle all the administrative bullshit that keeps you from spending quality time with the person you're caring for.

You shouldn't have to become an expert in Medicare, home health agencies, and medical terminology just to take care of someone you love.
